History of Pulau Sipadan, Sabah


Pulau Sipadan, Malaysia’s only oceanic island, was formed over thousands of years by living corals growing atop an extinct volcanic cone. Located in the Celebes Sea, the island rises 600 meters from the seabed, creating a unique and rich marine ecosystem.

In the early 1980s, Sipadan gained international attention as one of the world’s best diving destinations, thanks to Jacques Cousteau's documentary, Borneo: The Ghost of the Sea Turtle. Its stunning marine biodiversity, including turtles, sharks, and vibrant coral reefs, drew divers from around the globe.

In 2000, a territorial dispute over Sipadan between Malaysia and Indonesia was resolved by the International Court of Justice, which officially recognized Malaysia's sovereignty over the island. To preserve its delicate environment, all on-island resorts were removed in 2004, and the Malaysian government implemented strict conservation measures.

Today, Pulau Sipadan is a protected marine park, celebrated for its unparalleled beauty and ecological significance, attracting limited visitors each year to ensure its preservation.

What are they saying

Feel Sabah

Sipadan is well known for its unusually large numbers of green and hawksbill turtles which gather there to mate and nest and it is not unusual for a diver to see more than 20 turtles on each dive. More than 3000 species of fish and hundreds of coral species have been classified in this richest of ecosystems and thus made Sipadan an important marine habitat in this region.

Sabah Parks

The National Parks Ordinance of 1962 came into force on 11th May, 1962. The Ordinance provided for the constitution of national parks, following a Resident's enquiry into objections and claims for customary rights and the subsequent management of the national parks by a Board of Trustees.

Setiausaha Kerajaan Negeri Sabah

Sipadan has been named one of the best diving sites in the world. It is highly sought after by divers for its versatility. Sipadan Island looks like a mushroom (from a distant eye level view) offering 12 different dive points.

Ministry of Communications

The Malaysian Communications and Multimedia Commission (MCMC), with cooperation from the Sabah government through Sabah Parks, has identified the location for the construction of a telecommunication tower in Pulau Sipadan near here.

Amazing Borneo Tours

Sipadan Islands boasts as the perfect place for avid divers to who would like to add a tropical dive to their dive logs. The array of aquatic friends you’re able to make here are not of the average bunch for you will easily find yourself chasing manta rays, going deep for sharks, or off way into the blue for barracudas. Although Open Water Divers are permitted to traverse the waters of Sipadan, it is advised that one is confident during dives or at least have acquired their Advance Open Water dive certificate.
